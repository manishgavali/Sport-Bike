üèçÔ∏è Safe Riding Techniques

üéØ

Maintain Proper Position

Keep your body relaxed, grip the tank with your knees, and maintain a slight bend in your elbows. Look ahead where you want to go, not directly in front of your wheel. Your body position affects control and stability.

üõë

Master Braking Technique

Use both brakes together - apply 70% front brake and 30% rear brake for optimal stopping. Practice progressive braking (squeeze gradually) rather than grabbing. In emergencies, apply maximum pressure without locking wheels.

üîÑ

Smooth Cornering

Slow down before the turn, look through the corner to your exit point, and maintain steady throttle. Lean with the bike, not against it. Remember: Brake before the turn, accelerate through and out.

üëÅÔ∏è

Defensive Riding Mindset

Assume other drivers don't see you. Create space cushions around your bike. Constantly scan for potential hazards. Position yourself in lanes where you're most visible. Always have an escape route planned.

üö® In Case of Accident

  1. Move to a safe location if possible
  2. Call emergency services (108/112)
  3. Document the scene with photos
  4. Exchange information with other parties
  5. File a police report (required for insurance)
  6. Seek medical attention even for minor injuries
  7. Contact your insurance company within 24 hours

‚ö†Ô∏è Mechanical Breakdown

  1. Pull over safely and turn on hazard lights
  2. Move bike completely off the road if possible
  3. Place warning triangle 50-100m behind bike
  4. Assess the problem (if safe to do so)
  5. Call roadside assistance or mechanic
  6. Do not attempt complex repairs on roadside
  7. Wait in a safe location away from traffic
